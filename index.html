NeuroSPS: A Sensorimotor-Inspired Policy for Semantic, Proprioceptive, and Smooth Robotic Manipulation

Overall Framework of NeuroSPS.

Abstract

Overview: While Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models and imitation learning have unlocked complex robotic capabilities, existing end-to-end strategies bottleneck when it comes to precise manipulation. Issues like inefficient data preprocessing, disconnected macroscopic decisions from physical error correction, and high-frequency action jitter lead to progressive error accumulation. Inspired by the efficiency of human motor control, we introduce NeuroSPS, a framework designed for targeted modular optimization across data input, perception-decision, and execution stages.

Core Innovations:

  • SWR Downsampling Algorithm: By integrating a sliding window, semantic keypoint detection, and multi-order error compensation, SWR reduces computational complexity from O(n2) to O(kn). It compresses redundant data while perfectly retaining high-fidelity intention keyframes and trajectory smoothness. Performance Leap: Processing 1,500 real-world frames takes only 2-3 s/it, achieving an astonishing >1000x acceleration.
  • RT-DPC Net: Deeply fuses IMU features to dramatically enhance feedforward state estimation. During action generation, it introduces a temporal dynamic weighting mechanism tailored for active error correction during robot inference.
  • S2F Mechanism: A synergistic approach that combines training-phase temporal smoothing loss with inference-phase adaptive filtering to aggressively suppress compound errors caused by action jitter.

Exceptional Performance: When applied independently, SWR, RT-DPC Net, and S2F yield maximum success rate gains of 17%, 26%, and 18%, respectively. Evaluated across 12 rigorous simulation and real-world tasks, NeuroSPS achieves an average success rate of 71.5%. Boasting a 22% average performance improvement over the ACT baseline and outperforming mainstream strategies like InterACT, NeuroSPS significantly enhances robotic adaptability and stability in unstructured environments, paving an efficient modular path for autonomous manipulation.
